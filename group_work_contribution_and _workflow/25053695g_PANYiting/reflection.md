## How Did AI Tools Affect Your Production Pipeline?

**Before AI tools, this task would have required:**
- Building a full animation pipeline (storyboard drawing, keyframe design, animation, and compositing), which is difficult for one person to complete within a short course timeline.
- Repeated manual redesign of shots whenever the pacing or visual tone changed.
- More specialized software skills and significantly more time for each revision round.

**With AI tools (Jimeng AI + Lovart), the process became:**
- Prompt-driven production: I converted narrative beats across all video segments (Prologue, Boss2 CG, Ending A, and Ending B) into first-frame, last-frame, and motion prompts.
- Faster iteration cycles: I could generate multiple visual versions quickly and choose the one that best matched the emotional tone of each scene.
- More focus on narrative direction: my work shifted from manual execution to shot design, prompt precision, and quality selection.

**Unexpected challenges:**
- Character consistency across clips was harder than expected, especially costume details and facial expression continuity for all main characters.
- Camera movement outcomes were sometimes unstable, even when motion prompts were specific.
- Different generated clips had slight style drift in color and lighting, which increased post-selection and editing effort.

---

## What Were the Implications of Including AI Tools in Your Output?

**Creative implications:**  
AI tools expanded what I could attempt visually in a limited time. Because I handled all video segments, I could align visual language across different narrative phases: setup (Prologue), escalation (Boss2 CG), and dual-outcome endings (A/B). At the same time, creativity depended more on language clarity than drawing skill, so prompt writing became a core creative act.

**Quality implications:**  
The baseline visual quality was strong for a student project, and atmosphere-building was efficient. However, fine-grained control remained limited: subtle acting details, exact continuity between adjacent shots, and fully predictable camera behavior still required multiple retries and careful curation.

**Authorship implications:**  
I see the final output as human-led authorship with AI-assisted execution. I defined the scene goals, narrative rhythm, and acceptance criteria for each clip; the AI generated options; and I selected, refined, and assembled the final direction. The authorship is therefore curatorial and directorial rather than frame-by-frame handcrafted.

---

## Key Learnings

1. **Prompt precision determines output quality.** Specific descriptions of emotion, camera framing, and lighting consistently improved usable results.
2. **Consistency needs a reusable template.** Keeping stable style phrases and character descriptors across all prompts reduced visual drift.
3. **Selection is as important as generation.** Good results came from evaluating many outputs against story intention, not from single-pass generation.

---

## If I Did It Again…

I would prepare a stricter "character consistency kit" before generation, including fixed descriptor blocks for all characters, scene palettes, and emotional ranges, then reuse them in every prompt. I would also reserve more time for structured A/B testing of motion prompts across all video modules so camera language and pacing remain consistent from the prologue to both endings.
