# Reflection — Enos

**Member:** Shen Ziqi
**Character:** Enos · Ending B (Defeat)

---

## How Did AI Tools Affect Your Production Pipeline?

**Before AI tools, this task would have required:**
- A traditional animation workflow, demanding professional skills in character animation, background painting, and post-production compositing.
- Significant investment of time and manpower, especially for slow-motion, particle effects, and complex camera movements (like orbiting or dollying), which would need to be adjusted frame by frame.
- For a live-action approach, it would have involved building physical sets, actor performances, and expensive photography and lighting equipment.
- Additionally, the extensive storyboarding work would also increase costs.

**With AI tools, the process became:**
- A text-driven creative generation process. The core work shifted from "making" to "describing" and "directing"—that is, figuring out how to get the AI to understand and execute our creative vision through precise prompts.
- A drastically shortened cycle from concept to visual preview. We could generate multiple versions of a shot in a short amount of time, allowing for rapid iteration and selection, which is unimaginable in a traditional workflow.
- The ability to achieve surreal visual effects that are difficult to produce cheaply with conventional methods, such as the "world turning into food" scene.

**Unexpected challenges:**
- The AI's understanding of prompts was uncertain. The most typical example was when generating the "heroes fall" scene, the AI incorrectly distorted the characters' forms into other small bear-like figures, leading to an unsettling "uncanny valley" effect.
- Maintaining character and scene consistency was a huge challenge. The AI struggled to ensure uniformity in characters (like Enos's robe and glasses) and scene lighting across different shots.
- Precise control over cinematic language was very difficult. Although we could describe camera movements, the AI's execution was often unpredictable, requiring repeated trials to get close to the desired effect.

---

## What Were the Implications of Including AI Tools in Your Output?

**Creative implications:**
- AI tools acted as a "catalyst" and "amplifier" for creativity. They could quickly materialize the vague, grand ideas in our minds and inspire new ones.
- Sometimes, unexpected errors in the output could also spark new ideas, allowing us to embrace these "happy accidents" for more interesting results.
- The focus of creation shifted to storytelling and descriptive ability. A good AI creator first needs to be a good "writer" or "director" who can clearly construct a scene with words.

**Quality implications:**
- The output quality was unstable, exhibiting a "high floor, low ceiling" characteristic. The AI could quickly generate a "decent-looking" image, but achieving the polish, stability, and flawlessness of professional film and television is still very difficult. There were frequent flaws in details, logic, and continuity.

**Authorship implications:**
- The notion of authorship became blurred. Is the final work "my" creation or the AI's? I see it as a new form of "human-computer collaboration." The creator's role is more like a "director" or "curator"—we set the framework, provide direction, and then select, combine, and re-create from the vast number of results generated by the AI. Defining copyright and originality has become more complex.

---

## Key Learnings

1.  **Prompt Engineering is a Core Skill:** Learning how to write structured, detailed, and imaginative prompts is crucial. It's not just about translating a scene description but understanding the AI's "way of thinking."
2.  **Accept and Manage Unpredictability:** When using AI tools, you can't aim for 100% precise control. Instead, you have to learn to embrace randomness and either turn it into a part of the creative process or filter for the right results through extensive trials.
3.  **Documentation and Version Control are Important:** Detailed records of prompts for each shot, the results, and why they were discarded are invaluable for project review and learning. The `prompts.md` file we created is a testament to this practice.

---

## If I Did It Again…

I would invest more time in preliminary testing and prompt writing, systematically exploring the AI model's response to specific keywords (e.g., character names, specific actions, emotional expressions) to build a "controllability vocabulary." Because tokens are expensive, the goal would be to achieve the desired image in a single attempt. Additionally, I would try to perform more detailed editing, color grading, and compositing on the AI-generated clips in post-production software, rather than relying entirely on the AI's single-shot output, to improve the final quality and narrative coherence of the film.
